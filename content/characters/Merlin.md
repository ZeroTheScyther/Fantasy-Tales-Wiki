**Merlin** is one of the most powerful mages in [[Erinn]] — and one of the most mysterious. He cast his first Lightning Bolt at age three. Invented his first new spell at eleven. Mastered every known form of magic by twenty-three. The magic community expected much from him; he was made Royal Castle Druid and put to work on research and kingdom defence. The work lacked the challenge to hold his interest. He vanished in search of adventure. That was over a hundred years ago.

He hasn't aged a day. Reports persist: if you are ever in genuine need, a Druid fitting his description will appear. Ever the playful prankster.

## Inventions

Merlin didn't just master magic — he expanded what magic could do. His contributions are foundational:

- **Snap Cast:** Chargeable magic skills fire instantly — no casting time, no charge-up. Activates once per skill duration. The mechanic behind [[Zekito Silverblood|Zekito's]] instant Lightning Rod; the finger snap is the trigger. Considered impossible by Eorzean standards.
- **Spellwalk:** Allows spells to be cast while moving. Eorzean mages must be stationary to cast. [[Zekito Silverblood|Zekito]] arrived in Eorzea treating both as completely ordinary — he didn't realize they were unusual until people started staring.
- **Meteor Strike:** [[Zekito Silverblood|Zekito's]] LB3. His most destructive invention.
- **The Aces' Weapons:** Merlin crafted the tools used by [[The Aces]], including [[Zekito Silverblood|Zekito's]] custom knuckles — identical in shape to the ones he'd carried since age eleven, but built to channel high magic without a focus.

## Mentorship of Zekito

Merlin tested both [[Zekito Silverblood|Zekito]] and [[Mewecho]] before agreeing to enhance their bracelets. [[Mewecho]] returned to [[Audacia]]; [[Zekito Silverblood|Zekito]] spent four years travelling with [[The Aces]] under Merlin's mentorship. The training was rigorous and genuine — when Zekito left, Merlin gave him the custom knuckles as a farewell gift, without ceremony. The playful exterior and the real investment in other people's growth are both true.
